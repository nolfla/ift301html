Garden Gnomes

After World War II (with early references, in ironical use, from the late 1930s) the diminutive figurines introduced as lawn ornaments during the 19th century came to be known as garden gnomes. The image of the gnome changed further during the 1960s to 1970s, when the first plastic garden gnomes were manufactured. These gnomes followed the style of the 1937 depiction of the seven dwarves in Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs by Disney. This "Disneyfied" image of the gnome was built upon by the illustrated children's book classic The Secret Book of Gnomes (1976), in the original Dutch Leven en werken van de Kabouter. Garden gnomes share a resemblance to the Scandinavian tomte and nisse, and the Swedish term "tomte" can be translated to "gnome" in English.

Gnombre-- The Hispanic Garden Gnome

A Tempe-based enterprise run by University of Arizona grads is setting out to add diversity to the world of garden gnomes.

Meet Gnombre, the Hispanic bringer of whimsy to lawns, patios and gardens. Gnombre Inc., which is run by four people - three of them UA grads - is attempting to change the face of gnomedom by raising money for a production run via online investment service Kickstarter.

The group needs to collect contributions of $3,500 by March 18 to produce 500 gnomes. If the group doesn't hit its goal by the target date, backers get their money refunded.

Backers who kick in at least $30 will be assured a 13-inch Gnombre to call their own. Those who chip in $20 will get Gnombre's smaller, 7-inch bobblehead cousin. As of Wednesday, the project had received $2,778 from 58 investors.

Gnombre, who bares a slightly olive complexion, is decked out in a red sombrero, a serape tied up with an oversized belt buckle and mariachi pants.

We caught up with Duke Fonner, CEO of Symbius Financial, who runs the enterprise with longtime pal and fellow UA alum Aram Chavez, who teaches business classes at Northern Arizona University and Chandler-Gilbert Community College. Fonner's wife, academic counselor Vanessa Chavez - who is also Aram's sister - and Aram Chavez's wife, dentist Stephanie Walker, are the other partners.
